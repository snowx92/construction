# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Construction professionals in the GC (general contractor), specialty trade, and mid-market contracting space. Primary roles: estimators who build and review BOQs under deadline pressure; project managers tracking active jobs and vendor delivery; procurement leads comparing supplier pricing; company owners reviewing margins and proposal quality.

They use this seated at a desk or on a laptop in a site office, often with many browser tabs open, time-boxed before a client meeting or bid submission. Some access it on a tablet during site walks. The task is always clear: process a tender, make a decision, generate output.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered tender intelligence platform for construction companies. It does what estimators and proposal teams do manually, at 10x speed: analyzes tender PDFs, generates technical and financial proposals, compares vendor pricing against live market rates, detects contract risks, extracts BOQ quantities from drawings, and provides a conversational AI copilot with full context of the company's tenders, vendors, and pricing history.

Success: a contractor can upload a tender at 8am and have a complete, export-ready proposal by 9:30am instead of spending two days manually cross-referencing spreadsheets and PDFs.

## Brand Personality

Calm authority. Intelligent restraint. Precision without coldness.

Three words: precise, warm, intelligent.

The product should feel like working with a deeply competent colleague who never panics, never wastes your time, and gives you exactly what you need. Not a robot, not a dashboard, not a tool — a thinking partner.

## Anti-references

- SAP, Oracle, Procore (heavy, complex, enterprise ERP feel)
- Traditional construction PM software (Buildertrend, CoConstruct)
- Dense financial terminals
- Early-2010s SaaS dashboards with chart soup
- Anything that looks like a spreadsheet dressed up as a web app
- Heavy drop shadows, gradients on gradients, neon accents
- Glassmorphism used decoratively

## Design Principles

1. **The task disappears into the work.** Every interaction should feel so obvious that the tool becomes invisible. Users notice results, not UI.
2. **Calm is a feature.** Construction work is high-stakes and noisy. The interface should be a place of stillness and clarity. Never add visual urgency that isn't earned.
3. **AI earns its place.** AI surfaces appear when they add value, not to signal that AI exists. Never decorate with AI iconography or loading theatrics.
4. **Density on demand.** The default view is spacious and editorial. Power users can reveal more. Never default to maximum information density.
5. **Output is the product.** Every flow ends with something the user can use: a PDF, a comparison table, an exported proposal. Design toward the deliverable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Standard implementation: sufficient color contrast on all text and interactive elements, keyboard navigability on all flows, focus rings on all interactive targets, semantic HTML throughout. No special reduced-motion requirement, but Framer Motion animations should be proportional and non-disorienting.
